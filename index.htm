Internet Security

What the adults should know:

  • - Be aware of the risks and benifits of giving a child access to the internet
  • - Respect the privacy of your children on your own socials.
  • - Set boundaries for internet and screen time usage using parental controls.
  • - In a non-invasive way monitor young people's activity on the internet.

How to maintain security on the internet (The Basics):

  • - Never give out your personal information to people you don't know. This includes your name, address, phone number, and password.
  • - Be careful what websites you visit. Some websites can be phishing scams that steal your information (eg: gooogle.com, insttagram).
  • - Use a strong password only you know preferably containing uppercase letters and special characters. DO NOT SHARE SAID PASSWORD WITH ANYONE
  • - Be careful what you click on. Links in emails and messages can sometimes take you to dangerous websites.
  • - If something seems sketchy make sure to block and report it.

Here are some additional tips for staying safe on the internet:

  • - Keep your software up to date. Software updates often include security patches that can help protect you from vulnerabilities.
  • - Use an ad blocker on your browser, ads contain trackers that intrude on your right to privacy in order to advertise the "right" products to you and make more money.
  • - Be careful about what you download from the internet. Only download from trusted websites

and finally, here are some things to know:

  • - Know that the community on the internet isn't the only threat to internet security. The services you use harvest your data to train algorithms to make more money off you.
  • - Be careful about what you download from the internet. Only download files from websites that you trust.
  • - Be careful about what information you share on social media. Don't post anything you'll regret. What is on the internet stays on the internet

By following these tips, you can help keep yourself safe online.
